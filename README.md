# Digital-Literacy
Digital literacy refers to the ability to effectively and responsibly use digital technologies, such as computers, smartphones, the internet, and various software applications, to find, evaluate, create, and communicate information.
A digitally literate person can:

Search for information efficiently using search engines
Evaluate the credibility and reliability of online content
Use tools like word processors, spreadsheets, and presentation software
Communicate through emails, social media, and online platforms
Create digital content such as documents, images, and videos
Protect personal data and maintain online safety

Digital literacy also includes awareness of online ethics, cyber safety, and responsible digital behavior. This means understanding issues like privacy, cyberbullying, plagiarism, and digital footprints.
Importance of Digital Literacy:

Enhances learning and research skills
Improves employability and workplace efficiency
Promotes safe and responsible internet use
Helps individuals stay informed and connected
Encourages creativity and innovation
