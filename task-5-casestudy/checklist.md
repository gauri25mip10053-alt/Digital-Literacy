# Stay Safe Online – Cybercrime Prevention Checklist (India)

## Actionable Tips for College Students

* Use strong, unique passwords for each account and enable 2-factor authentication (2FA).
*  Never share OTPs, PINs, passwords, or CVV details with anyone—even if they claim to be from a bank.
*  void clicking on unknown links in emails, SMS, or WhatsApp messages (phishing scams).
*   Install apps only from official stores like Google Play Store or Apple App Store.
*  Keep your phone, laptop, and apps updated to protect against security vulnerabilities.
*   void using public Wi-Fi for logging into banking, email, or social media accounts.
*  Always log out from shared or public computers (college labs, cyber cafes).
* Review and limit app permissions (camera, contacts, location) regularly.

##  Financial / UPI Safety Tips

* Double-check UPI IDs and QR codes before making payments—scammers can fake them.
* Do not accept “collect requests” from unknown persons on UPI apps (they may be scams).
* Remember: Receiving money does NOT require entering your UPI PIN—only sending money does.
* Enable transaction alerts and set daily limits on your banking/UPI apps.

##  Report Cybercrime Immediately

-  National Cyber Crime Portal: https://cybercrime.gov.in
-  Helpline Number: 1930

Report fraud as soon as possible to increase the chances of recovering your money.
